What's to come?

I think I'm a person who generally prefers knowing over not knowing something. But when it comes to the future I'm a bit torn.
While I value predictability, it can also make the experience less interesting and exciting. I love being surprised and learning new information.
Knowing beforehand or being too invested in one particular path, it sort of removes what I think is fun in life.
I often reflect upon events and choices from the perspective that my universe is one of infinitely many.
Every version of my actions is happening, it's up to me to choose the one that leads to my prefered outcome.
It's a difficult thing to explain, but enough existential philosophy for now.

That being said, I do have some goals I'd like to achieve in life.
I want to know a lot about code and how to manipulate machines. I intend to stay humble and adapt to new technology as I get older.
I wish to preserve my active physical lifestyle and to never lose the will to learn further.
I'd like to somehow contribute to the improvement of human life, both locally and globally.
Such so that when I'm done living, I'd like for all my good deeds to have far outweighed the bad.
If the consequenses of me existing (however small they may be) has had a positive effect on the world, then I am more than content.

Most of all I hope to be happy and to be surrounded by good people.
