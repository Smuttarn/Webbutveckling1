The story so far

Early one Friday morning in 1994, a child was born.
'Twas me.

Swedish became my third language, thanks to my Finnish mother and Chilean father. I am very thankful of my upbringing and what my parents did for us.
My sister and I were encouraged to pursue happiness in our lives, while showing acceptance and respect towards others.
Our household always had laughter as a main ingredient, which I try to bring with me wherever I go.

Through kindergarten to high-school I had trouble knowing what I would want to do with my life. No subject felt particularly interesting for me to want to pursue.
The only constant thing was my fascination for languages. Later during the course of my education I took a liking to learning and comparing them.
I began exploring languages with roots in Latin, in which Spanish helped med immensely and the learning process was facilitated thanks to similarities in grammar.
I reflected upon how I was getting used to the process of learning a new language. I'd break it up into steps and exposed myself to situations in which I'd be forced to try and speak.
It was during my time attempting to learn Mandarin that I began thinking of other types of languages, mainly those that were used to communicate with machines...
